Here’s your **7-day study plan** for the "Complex Numbers - I" chapter. This plan ensures a balance between theory, numerical practice, and revision, keeping in mind exam pressure and a realistic time commitment.

---

### **Day 1: Fundamental Concepts and Basic Properties**
- **Topics to Cover:**
  1. Understanding iota (\( i = sqrt(-1) \)) and powers of \( i \).
  2. Representing a complex number (\( z = a + ib \)).
  3. Conjugates and equality in complex numbers.
  4. Conjugate Complex Properties:
     - \( z + \bar{z} = 2\mathrm{Re}(z) \), \( z - \bar{z} = 2i\mathrm{Im}(z) \),
     - \( z\bar{z} = a^2 + b^2 \) (real).
- **Study Approach:**
  - **Theory:** 2 hours
    - Understand definitions and derivations.
    - Focus on relationship between \( z \) and \( \bar{z} \).
  - **Numerical Practice:** 1 hour
    - Solve exercise problems related to basic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication) and verifying properties.
- **Total Time:** 3 hours

---

### **Day 2: Modulus & Properties**
- **Topics to Cover:**
  1. Modulus of a complex number (\( |z| \)).
  2. Important properties of modulus:
     - \( |z| \geq 0 \), \( |z| \geq \mathrm{Re}(z) \), \( |z| \geq \mathrm{Im}(z) \), etc.
     - \( |z|^2 = z \cdot \bar{z} \), triangle inequalities.
- **Study Approach:**
  - **Theory:** 1.5 hours
    - Understand how the modulus represents the length of the vector.
    - Thoroughly examine all the modulus properties.
  - **Numerical Practice:** 1.5 hours
    - Practice problems derived from modulus properties.
    - Solve typical modulus comparison and triangle inequality questions.
- **Total Time:** 3 hours

---

### **Day 3: Amplitude & Polar Form of Complex Numbers**
- **Topics to Cover:**
  1. Amplitude (\( \arg(z) \)) of complex numbers and its significance.  
  2. Relationship between amplitude and modulus.
  3. Conversion between rectangular and polar form \( z = r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta) \).
  4. Important amplitude properties.
- **Study Approach:**
  - **Theory:** 1.5 hours
    - Understand how amplitude provides the angle of the complex number vector.
    - Explore polar representation and its benefits (e.g., trigonometric multiplication rules).
  - **Numerical Practice:** 1.5 hours
    - Practice converting between rectangular and polar forms.
    - Solve amplitude problems and verify amplitude properties.
- **Total Time:** 3 hours

---

### **Day 4: De Moivre’s Theorem and Cube Roots of Unity**
- **Topics to Cover:**
  1. **De Moivre’s Theorem** with integer and rational powers.
  2. Application of De Moivre’s Theorem to problems involving powers and roots.
  3. **Cube roots of unity**:
     - \( 1, \omega = -\frac{1}{2} + i\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}, \omega^2 = -\frac{1}{2} - i\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \).
     - Properties: \( 1 + \omega + \omega^2 = 0 \), \( x^2 + x + 1 = 0 \).
- **Study Approach:**
  - **Theory:** 2 hours
    - Derive and understand De Moivre’s formula (case I and case II).
    - Learn the properties of cube roots of unity.
  - **Numerical Practice:** 2 hours
    - Solve problems involving De Moivre’s Theorem for powers and roots.
    - Work on properties of cube roots of unity (e.g., algebraic expansions).
- **Total Time:** 4 hours
